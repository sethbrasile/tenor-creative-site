# Project Research Summary

**Project:** Tenor Creative Redesign
**Domain:** Digital Agency for SMBs (Web, SEO, CRM)
**Researched:** Jan 25 2026
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Executive Summary

The Tenor Creative Redesign aims to transform the agency's digital presence from a generalist service provider to a focused "Guide" for overwhelmed small business owners. Research confirms that the target audience values simplicity, trust, and clear outcomes (leads, visibility) over technical jargon or complex service menus. The "Hero" of the story is the client, not the agency; Tenor's role is the empathic guide with a plan.

The recommended approach is to adopt the **StoryBrand "Guide" framework** for all messaging, bundling services into clear **"Essentials" packages** (Brochure Web, Local SEO, CRM) rather than selling à la carte technical services. The site architecture should be flattened and problem-focused (e.g., "Get Found" instead of "SEO Services"), driving all traffic toward a frictionless "Book Strategy Call" conversion loop.

Key risks include the "Tech Flex" (alienating users with developer jargon), clinging to internal frameworks (e.g., "The 4 Rs") that confuse users, and the "Full Service" trap of offering too much. Mitigating these requires ruthless simplification of the sitemap, strict adherence to benefit-driven copy, and high-friction form removal.

## Key Findings

### Messaging Strategy (The "Guide" Model)

[Summary from MESSAGING.md]

**Core Framework:** StoryBrand ("You are the Hero, We are the Guide").
- **Tone:** Professional, Simple, Essential.
- **Key Phrase:** "We handle the tech, you handle the business."
- **Avoid:** "Full-stack", "React", "Synergy", "Bespoke".

### Service Packaging (The "Essentials")

[Summary from PACKAGING.md]

**Productization:** Move from "Web Design" -> "Digital Storefront Systems".

**Must have (The Starter):**
- 5-Page Structure (Home, About, Services, Contact, Blog)
- Mobile Responsiveness & SSL
- Google Business Profile setup

**Should have (The Growth Engine):**
- Local SEO Setup (Citations, Keywords)
- CRM Lite (Missed Call Text Back, Review Automation)

**Defer / Anti-Features:**
- Custom E-commerce (complex scope creep)
- "Pixel Perfect" custom design (kills margins)

### Site Flows & Structure

[Summary from FLOWS.md]

**Architecture:** Flat hierarchy focused on user problems.
- **Nav:** Solutions (Get Found, Get Leads) > AI Demo > Pricing > About > Contact.
- **Primary CTA:** "Book Strategy Call" (Sticky nav, Hero, Footer).
- **Secondary CTA:** "Hear AI Demo" (High-interest hook).

### Critical Pitfalls

[Top 3 from PITFALLS.md]

1.  **The "Tech Flex":** showcasing stack details instead of business benefits. *Fix: Translate every feature to a benefit.*
2.  **Internal Jargon ("The 4 Rs"):** Using agency methodologies as navigation. *Fix: Rename to industry-standard terms (SEO, Leads).*
3.  **Form Friction:** Asking 10+ questions upfront. *Fix: Email/Phone only, then qualify later.*

## Implications for Roadmap

Based on research, suggested phase structure:

### Phase 1: Strategy & Definition
**Rationale:** Must define the "Guide" voice and "Essentials" packages before writing or building.
**Delivers:** Brand Voice Guide, Service Tier Definitions, Final Pricing Model.
**Addresses:** Messaging strategy, Service packaging.
**Avoids:** "Full Service" trap.

### Phase 2: Content & Copywriting
**Rationale:** Content drives design. Copy must be written first to ensure the "Guide" tone is consistent.
**Delivers:** Homepage Copy, Solution Page Copy ("Get Found", "Get Leads"), Lead Magnet content.
**Uses:** StoryBrand Framework, PAS Formula.
**Avoids:** "Tech Flex" copy, "We-centric" language.

### Phase 3: Core Site Structure & Design
**Rationale:** Implementing the flattened sitemap and visual hierarchy based on the new content.
**Delivers:** Home, Solution Pages, Pricing Page, About.
**Implements:** "Solutions" Dropdown, Sticky CTA Nav.
**Avoids:** "Dead End" service pages.

### Phase 4: Conversion Engine & Integrations
**Rationale:** Connecting the high-value funnels once the structure is in place.
**Delivers:** "Book Strategy Call" flow (GHL Calendar), "Missed Call" widget, AI Voice Demo integration.
**Implements:** Lead Magnet Funnel, Direct Booking Flow.
**Avoids:** Form friction (complex forms).

### Phase Ordering Rationale
- **Strategy First:** Packaging determines the pages needed.
- **Content Second:** Design should house the copy, not squeeze it in.
- **Structure Third:** Building the pages based on the finalized sitemap.
- **Conversion Last:** hooking up the plumbing once the "storefront" is ready.
