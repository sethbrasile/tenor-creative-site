# Build Brief: tenorcreative.com — Tenor Creative LLC (technical build studio)

**Read this first:** This document gives you the *goals, requirements, supporting
facts, and copy direction* for a website. It deliberately says **nothing about visual
design, layout, color, or components** — those decisions are 100% yours. Make it fast,
credible, and distinctive.

Two rules about the writing:
1. **Write the copy yourself.** Below are angles, value props, and raw facts — not
   finished sentences. Do **not** paste my phrasings verbatim. Follow the Voice Rules (§4).
2. **Everything factual must come from §6 (The Data).** Don't invent clients, metrics,
   or credentials. If a fact isn't in §6, leave it out.

> **This is a repositioning, not a recreation.** The current tenorcreative.com sells
> marketing / customer-acquisition ("Double Your Business," get leads, reviews, sales).
> That is now the job of the sister agency **PPMC** and must be removed here. Tenor's
> new identity is the **technical build arm**: custom apps, automation, AI tooling,
> systems/infra, and technical consulting.

## 1. What we're building & why

A B2B site for **Tenor Creative LLC** at **tenorcreative.com**. Tenor is the
**custom-software, automation, and systems** company. Its founder and principal is
**Seth Brasile**.

**The ecosystem (build the positioning around this):**
- **PPMC (pricklypearmarketing.co)** = web, SEO, and marketing.
- **Tenor Creative** = custom apps, systems, automation, AI tooling, technical
  consulting. Tenor is the technical subcontractor *behind* PPMC and other agencies.

**Primary goal — win technical work, channel-first:**

1. **Primary audience = agencies & partners** who need a **white-label technical

2. **Secondary audience = direct clients** (founders, SMBs, ops leaders) who need
   custom software or automation that off-the-shelf can't do. Same contact path.

**Cross-links (important for the ecosystem + SEO):**
- "Need marketing, SEO, or a website instead?" → link out to **PPMC
  (pricklypearmarketing.co)**.
- "Who's behind Tenor?" → link to **sethbrasile.com** (founder).

**What success looks like:** an agency owner or founder lands, immediately gets that
Tenor is senior technical execution they can trust and (for agencies) put their name
on, sees proof, and books a conversation.

## 2. Audiences & their journeys

- **Agency owners / agency PMs (PRIMARY)** — need to deliver custom builds,
  automation, or infra they can't staff in-house. Care about: senior execution (no
  offshore juniors), white-label / works under *their* brand, won't poach their
  clients, NDA-friendly, reliable comms and timelines, fills the technical gap beyond
  web/marketing. The PPMC relationship is living proof.
- **Founders / SMB owners / ops leaders (SECONDARY)** — have a problem off-the-shelf
  software can't solve, or manual work eating their team. Care about: does this person
  actually understand my business, will it get built and run reliably, what's the proof.
- **Technical evaluators (CTOs, technical co-founders)** — vetting competence and
  judgment. Care about: stack, architecture decisions, honesty about tradeoffs.

Make the **agency / white-label partner** path the most prominent, with a clear
secondary path for direct clients. One shared contact/booking action is fine.

## 4. Voice & copy rules

Voice = the founder's: **confident, concrete, technically literate, zero fluff.** Use a
**Tenor brand voice with Seth as the named principal** — first-person ("I") is right in
the About / founder section; "we" is acceptable when describing capacity/delivery.
Don't pretend to be a big faceless team.

**Do:** concrete specifics (real tools, scale, team sizes, dollar figures); plain
strong verbs (built, shipped, designed, led, architected, secured, automated); honest
scope (POC vs production); lead with outcomes.

**Don't (banned):** no "passionate about," "rockstar," "synergy," "leverage,"
"spearhead," "results-driven," "detail-oriented," "team player," "responsible for,"
"helped," or "various/multiple" without a number. No buzzword-salad headers. If a line
sounds like a generic agency cliché, rewrite it.

Tone: technical buyers (agency principals, founders, CTOs) — you can be more technical
here than a marketing site, but stay clear and outcome-focused. Trust and reliability
are the emotional core for the agency audience.

## 7. SEO requirements

This site's SEO job: rank for technical-services and white-label/subcontractor intent,
own the "Tenor Creative" brand result, and interlink the three properties.

- **Unique `<title>` + meta description per page;** one clear `<h1>`; logical heading
  hierarchy.
- **Target topics / intents** (weave in naturally): white-label software development /
  development subcontractor for agencies; custom software & SaaS development; business
  process automation; AI tooling / agents & integrations; systems / DevOps / fractional
  CTO; "off-the-shelf can't do it" custom builds; Durant OK / remote.
- **Interlink the ecosystem with dofollow links:** Tenor ↔ PPMC
  (pricklypearmarketing.co) ↔ sethbrasile.com. This authority cluster benefits all
  three. Link to the founder portfolio and to PPMC from relevant body copy.
- Clean URLs (e.g. /services, /work, /partners or /agencies, /contact); XML sitemap;
  robots.txt; canonical tags.
- **Fast Core Web Vitals** — Tenor sells technical quality; the site must demonstrate
  it (optimize LCP/CLS/INP, lazy-load below-the-fold, compress images, minimal blocking JS).
- Descriptive, keyword-aware `alt` text; Open Graph + Twitter Card with a preview image
  per page.
- **Privacy-friendly analytics** (Plausible preferred — consistent with byteMyCache).
